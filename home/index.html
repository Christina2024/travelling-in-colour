Hi Chicas!

Welcome to my blog where solo-travelling in colour meets community! This community is built upon the interests and some curiousity (as newbies) for solo travelling as women of colour.

To date, I have solo-travelled to over 10 countries, including Morocco, Portugal, and Egypt, Greece, amongst others. Ironically, my fear of flying is what pushed me to embark on these adventures alone. In the past, traveling with friends and family made me realize how much I relied on others to navigate unfamiliar places. By stepping out of my comfort zone, I've not only conquered my fear of flights but also gained a strong sense of independence. Exploring different cultures on my own has enriched my understanding of the world, improved my social skills, and given me the confidence to handle any challenge that comes my way.

Solo travelling especially as a woman can come with risks, not to mention if you're a woman of colour, there can be some locations that may come with general safety precautions, and social qeues -especially staring, to be concerned of in some countries. Which is why I created this space for us women to empower, encourage, and support each other. Solo-travelling has helped me to face challenges and gain life lessons.
You never know whats on the other side of life until you take the journey by the horns!
So let's go!

Love Annitta ♡

Solo travel 101

The thought of solo-travelling can be overwhelming for most. However, what it has shown me is a new form of reconnecting with myself and connecting with the communities I travel to. Travelling alone as a woman, I recommend starting out small first! This looks like:
  • Taking yourself out to eat alone
  • Visiting surrounding domestic cities by yourself
  • Going to the cinemas by yourself
    • I used to do this all the time before making my first international solo trip, and it allowed me to build up confidence in navigating the world alone.

    You can never be too prepared when traveling alone. Be sure to conduct thorough research about the places you visit. This includes crime rates, weather, other solo travel experiences from the area, what to do, nightlife, and basically everything you can think of. I recommend watching YouTube videos to get an authentic feel for a destination you will be seeing or want to see in the future.
